Well-being & Basic Needs

Welcome to the Well-being & Basic Needs resource hub. College life can be overwhelming, and success isn’t just about academics—it’s about taking care of yourself physically, mentally, and emotionally. This page connects you with key non-academic resources at U-M that support your mental health, food security, and overall well-being.

Why These Resources Are Important

  • Mental Health Matters - Stress, anxiety, and burnout are common among students. Accessing professional counseling and wellness resources can make a huge difference.
  • No student should go hungry - Many students face food insecurity. The Maize and Blue Cupboard provides free food and essentials so you can focus on your studies without worrying about your next meal.
  • You're not alone - U-M offers a range of support systems to help you thrive inside and outside the classroom.

What You'll Find on This Page

  • CAPS (Counseling and Psychological Services): Learn about mental health resources, crisis support, and counseling options.
  • Maize and Blue Cupboard Access free food, personal care items, and learn how to get involved.
  • Additional Support: Links to the Student Life Basic Needs Hub for financial aid, housing support, and more.

CAPS: Mental Health and Counseling

What is CAPS?

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) provides free, confidential mental health support to U-M students. Whether you're feeling overwhelmed, stressed, or just need someone to talk to, CAPS is here to help.

Services Offered

  • Individual & Group Counseling – Personalized support for stress, anxiety, depression, etc.
  • Workshops & Wellness Coaching – Programs on mindfulness, self-care, and academic stress management.
  • Crisis & Urgent Support – 24/7 mental health crisis services (link to U-M’s crisis resources).
  • Links to the Student Life Basic Needs Hub for financial aid, housing support, and more. If you are experiencing a crisis, you can reach out to CAPS at (734) 764-8312 or visit their crisis support page.

How to Access CAPS

  • Location: – Michigan Union, 4th Floor
  • Hours: – Monday - Friday, 8 am - 5pm
  • Appointments – Walk-in for urgent concerns or schedule online.
  • Include CAPS phone number & emergency support options.

Maize and Blue Cupboard

What is the Maize and Blue Cupboard?

The Maize and Blue Cupboard is a free food and personal care pantry for U-M students. It aims to alleviate food insecurity and provide essential items to help students succeed.

Services Offered

  • Free Food & Essentials: Access non-perishable food, personal care items, and household supplies.
  • Pop-Up Pantries: Monthly events across campus to distribute food and resources.
  • Get Involved: Volunteer opportunities and ways to donate food or funds.

How to Access the Maize and Blue Cupboard

  • Location: Betsy Barbour Residence Hall, 420 S. State St.
  • Hours: Monday - Friday, 10 am - 6 pm
  • Eligibility: Open to all U-M students with a valid MCard.

How to Get Involved

  • Volunteer: Help sort and distribute food at the pantry.
  • Donate: Contribute non-perishable food items or make a financial donation.
  • Spread the Word: Share information about the pantry with fellow students.
